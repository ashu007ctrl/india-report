Pahalgam Terror Attack in Kashmir

Published Date : August 18, 2025

The Attack

On April 22, 2025, five heavily armed terrorists affiliated with Lashkar-e-Taiba and The Resistance Front opened fire on tourists in the Baisaran Valley near Pahalgam, killing 26 civilians and injuring 20 more. The attack was one of the deadliest since 2008, primarily targeting Hindu pilgrims.

Aftermath and Response

Prime Minister Narendra Modi condemned the attack and promised swift justice. Security forces launched extensive manhunts, while over 2,000 individuals were detained for investigation. One terrorist’s house was demolished, raising concerns about collective punishment.

India-Pakistan Tensions Escalate

India accused Pakistan of backing the terrorists, leading to diplomatic fallout, suspension of water treaties, and increased military activity along the border. Pakistan denied involvement, urging international mediation.

International Reaction

The United States and other global powers expressed concern. Diplomatic outreach from U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio and VP JD Vance focused on preventing further escalation.

Impact on Kashmir

The attack dealt a severe blow to Kashmir’s tourism industry. Local entrepreneurs expressed fear and disappointment over potential long-term consequences for the region’s fragile peace.

Conclusion

As of now, the attackers remain at large. This incident has reignited debates on national security, Indo-Pak relations, and the future of peace in Kashmir.
