BJP Welfare Schemes (2025)

Published Date : August 18, 2025

Flagship welfare and development programmes under BJP-led governments span clean cooking, healthcare, housing, sanitation, farmer support, skilling, manufacturing, and digital public infrastructure. Here’s a concise, human-first explainer of the big schemes, their design logic, and on‑ground impact narratives.

1. Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)

PMUY enables women from low-income households to access LPG connections, replacing smoky solid fuels with cleaner cooking. The programme’s core benefit is health and dignity—time saved, soot reduced, and safer kitchens. Subsequent phases supported refills and expanded last‑mile distribution to deepen usage.

2. Ayushman Bharat – PM Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY)

PMJAY provides cashless hospitalization cover up to ₹5 lakh per eligible family per year for secondary and tertiary care. Paired with Health & Wellness Centres for primary care, the architecture aims to cut catastrophic health expenditure and improve access in small towns and rural geographies.

3. Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

PMAY supports affordable housing through assistance and interest subsidies for eligible urban and rural beneficiaries. The focus is a pucca home with basic amenities, often leveraging newer, faster, and more sustainable construction technologies in partnership with states and urban local bodies.

4. Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM)

SBM accelerated household and community toilet coverage and nudged behaviour change on sanitation and waste management. The larger public-health payoff: fewer waterborne diseases, cleaner commons, and a visible shift in cleanliness norms across schools, public spaces, and transport hubs.

5. PM-KISAN

PM-KISAN offers direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to eligible farmers in three instalments, designed to ease liquidity constraints for seasonal inputs and household needs. Timely transfers via digital rails help smooth consumption and support smallholder resilience.

6. Make in India

Make in India aims to expand domestic manufacturing capacity, attract investment, and enable job creation across priority sectors like electronics, defence, and autos. Combined with process reforms, infrastructure upgrades, and PLI‑style incentives, it seeks to deepen supply chains and lift exports.

7. Digital India

Digital India builds foundational digital public infrastructure: identity, payments, data exchanges, and e‑governance services. With UPI, DigiLocker, and Aadhaar-enabled systems, the goal is simple, secure, low‑cost access to public services and the formal economy—especially for first‑time users.

8. Jal Jeevan Mission (Rural)

Focused on household tap water connections in rural areas, this mission targets safe and regular supply, water quality testing, and community participation. Reliable access reduces health risks and time burdens, particularly for women and children.

Conclusion

From clean fuel to cashless care and digital rails, these programmes share a common thread: scale with targeting, and delivery through increasingly digitized, direct‑to‑beneficiary systems. The next leap hinges on quality of service, accountability, and resilient public infrastructure.
