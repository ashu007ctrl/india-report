Thailand–Cambodia Conflict 2025

Published July 30, 2025

Why It Flared Up in 2025

The dispute around the Preah Vihear temple and adjacent high ground remains a flashpoint. While the ICJ awarded the temple to Cambodia in 1962, sovereignty over surrounding approaches is contested. In early 2025, new Thai fortifications along disputed ridgelines and Cambodian patrols in buffer areas triggered reciprocal accusations of ceasefire violations.

Nationalist narratives on both sides—territorial redress for Thailand versus existential sovereignty for Cambodia—complicate de‑escalation. Local dynamics such as illegal logging, smuggling routes, and revenue control in remote border forests add friction and provide cover for armed groups and militias.

Force Balance and Tactics

Thailand fields one of the region’s more capable militaries, with roughly 360,000 active personnel, legacy and upgraded armor, M109A5 howitzers, and a combat‑credible air arm (e.g., F‑16s) plus emerging subsurface capabilities. Combined arms and air‑land coordination give Bangkok an edge in conventional engagements and logistics control at key passes.

Cambodia, with ~125,000 personnel, has modernized select units via Chinese systems (new armor, UAVs, air‑defense and radar kits). Its doctrine emphasizes terrain denial, dispersed artillery, and light infantry operating in dense jungle to offset Thai advantages. Expect ambushes, counter‑battery harassment, and information ops alongside cyber probes from both sides.

Border skirmishes and artillery exchanges have produced casualties and displacement near contested villages, while both capitals amplify competing narratives in regional media and online spaces.

Thailand–U.S. Security Cooperation

A longstanding U.S. treaty ally, Thailand regularly trains with Washington via Cobra Gold and related exercises. In this crisis, the U.S. urges restraint but maintains advisory channels and limited intelligence sharing. Any support is calibrated to avoid direct entanglement while signaling commitment to alliance obligations and regional stability.

Cambodia–China Strategic Ties

Phnom Penh’s deepening partnership with China includes infrastructure financing and security assistance. Activity around Ream Naval Base has heightened scrutiny, with reports of Chinese presence fueling concern over broader Indo‑Pacific implications. Chinese UAVs, sensors, and logistics support improve Cambodian situational awareness and quick‑reaction capacity.

Critics warn of proxy dynamics if great‑power competition hardens, though both Beijing and Washington publicly prefer de‑escalation to protect trade flows and ASEAN stability.

Scenarios and Diplomatic Track

ASEAN faces pressure to mediate, but consensus is difficult. Shuttle diplomacy and backchannels—potentially involving Indonesia, Singapore, and Vietnam—aim to restore disengagement lines and incident‑prevention protocols. A UN call for ceasefire monitoring could follow if border incidents persist.

Escalation risks include prolonged artillery duels, miscalculation near civilian areas, and disruption to overland trade arteries. A viable off‑ramp would combine verified pullbacks, demarcation talks, and localized economic arrangements to reduce smuggling incentives.
