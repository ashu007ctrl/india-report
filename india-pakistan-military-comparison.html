India vs Pakistan Military Comparison 2025

Published Date: August 18, 2025

India and Pakistan continue to field sizable, professional armed forces. This 2025 overview contrasts their military balance—covering personnel, spending, and the major combat arms—while noting the stabilizing role of nuclear deterrence and the region’s evolving security calculus.

Manpower

  • India: ~1.45 million active personnel; ~1.15 million reserves
  • Pakistan: ~654,000 active personnel; ~550,000 reserves

Defense Budget (2025)

  • India: ~$78 billion (modernization, indigenization, and infrastructure)
  • Pakistan: ~$11 billion (force sustainment and incremental upgrades)

Land Forces

  • India: 4,500+ tanks; 9,000+ armored vehicles; 4,000+ artillery systems
  • Pakistan: 3,400+ tanks; 2,500+ armored vehicles; 2,000+ artillery systems

Air Force

  • India: 2,200+ total aircraft (including Su‑30MKI, Rafale, Mirage 2000, Tejas)
  • Pakistan: 1,300+ total aircraft (including JF‑17, F‑16, Mirage III/V)

Naval Power

  • India: 1 aircraft carrier; 10 destroyers; 20 frigates; 15 submarines
  • Pakistan: No carrier; ~10 frigates; 8 submarines

Nuclear Deterrence

  • India: Estimated 160–170 warheads; declared no‑first‑use posture
  • Pakistan: Estimated 165–175 warheads; first‑use option retained

Bottom Line

India holds clear advantages in resource base, budgets, and force structure breadth, while Pakistan maintains a credible deterrent and emphasizes asymmetric and rapid‑response capabilities. Both continue modernization efforts as strategic dynamics in South Asia evolve.
